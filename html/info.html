Useful Info

Vegan Hiking Boots

This site is run by an individual called Jess, she writes reviews on all sorts of brands/products recpes / walks etc - A great site to sit and have a read with a hot cuppa.

Veganuary

Veganuary – Is not just for January - This has been the most helpful site I have found and they even do a vegan starter kit...

The Vegan Society

This is a top place to start if you are looking for information on veganism. It’s a registered charity that’s been around for years – so there is a large amount of information available.

The Butchers Son

This place opened up in 2016 and they make all of their own meats and cheeses in house. I’ve heard that the fried mozzarella is amazing, they do cheesecake which I have missed so much since going vegan & the bacon tastes like real bacon if you are into that sort of thing. Apparently it gets really busy with queues out of the door – always a good sign, but not good if you are really hungry.

The Fying Falafel – San Fransisco

They serve vegan cheesecake- say no more. Just kidding. This place sounds great if you just want a quick take-away street food – some of the other places I have checked out seem to be really popular and very busy. They get really good reviews so I thought I’d add it to my list as I really like falafel and hummus and they also serve cinnamon baklava if you want a sweet treat afterwards.

Vegenation

They have a large range of dishes to choose from – Italian/Mexican/Vietnamese and more. There is even an option called the “Pamela Anderson” which I am slightly intrigued by! Oh and they do cocktails! Whoop.
